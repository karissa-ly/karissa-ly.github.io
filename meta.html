This Portfolio
Is the Case Study

I tried building a portfolio from a template years ago. It never felt like me, so I abandoned it.

This one started as an experiment. I was playing around with Lovable for an assignment and thought I'd try generating a portfolio layout while I was at it. It kind of worked, but Lovable goes high-fidelity fast, and I kept wanting to change things it wasn't built to change easily. Because of this, I moved into Claude and started writing the code myself, with Claude doing most of the heavy lifting and me directing it.

The node-link diagram was the part I was most excited about. During my first term at City, my data visualisation lecturer Professor Jo Wood showed us some work from the Information is Beautiful Awards — including Roots of Racism by TAAF, which was my first proper encounter with scrollytelling, and I was immediately obsessed. Later in the module, he showed us node-link diagrams as a way of visualising networks and relationships. I wanted to use the same idea to visualise myself: skills, tools, interests as a network rather than a list. Data visualisation is a side passion of mine. It's something I genuinely almost pursued further, so putting it at the centre of the homepage felt right.

Everything on this site is something I made, learned from, or lived through. This is a live document — I'll keep building it.
